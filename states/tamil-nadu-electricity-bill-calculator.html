How to Calculate Your Bill With Subsidy

Example: 300 Units Consumed (Bi-Monthly)

  1. First 100 units: 100 units x ₹0 = ₹0 (Subsidy)
  2. Next 100 units (101-200): 100 units x ₹2.25 = ₹225
  3. Next 100 units (201-300): 100 units x ₹4.50 = ₹450
  4. Total Energy Charge: ₹0 + ₹225 + ₹450 = ₹675
  5. Add Fixed Charges: Assuming ₹50. So, ₹675 + ₹50 = ₹725 (Approx.)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does 1 unit of electricity cost in Tamil Nadu?

For domestic consumers in Tamil Nadu, the first 100 units are free per bi-monthly cycle. After that, the cost per unit increases with consumption slabs. For example, units from 101-200 cost ₹2.25/unit, and units from 201-400 cost ₹4.50/unit.

How is the TNEB/TANGEDCO electricity bill calculated?

The TNEB bill is calculated on a slab system for the total units consumed in a bi-monthly cycle. The total energy charge is the sum of charges from each slab, plus fixed charges. Our calculator automates this entire process for you.

What is the 100 units free subsidy scheme in Tamil Nadu?

The primary subsidy provides the first 100 units of electricity for free to all domestic consumers (LT-1A) for their bi-monthly billing cycle. This is applied directly to the bill.

What is the electricity billing cycle in Tamil Nadu?

The electricity billing cycle for most domestic consumers in Tamil Nadu is bi-monthly, meaning a bill is generated once every two months.

Energy Saving Tips for Tamil Nadu

Optimize AC Use

Given Tamil Nadu's warm climate, AC is a major expense. Keep it at 24-26°C and ensure regular servicing to maintain efficiency and reduce your bi-monthly bill.

Use Ceiling Fans Smartly

A fan can make a room feel cooler, allowing you to raise the AC temperature. Using fans and AC together is more energy-efficient than running the AC at a very low temperature.

Unplug Electronics

Devices like TVs, chargers, and set-top boxes consume 'phantom power' even when off. Unplug them when not in use to save a few units on every bill.
